**Project Report**

*The Cost of a Misinterpreted Message*

In a recent incident involving a student study group in Bengaluru, a sarcastic joke made by a student in a WhatsApp group was taken as a direct threat by another peer. The message, stripped of tone and context, led to a formal college disciplinary hearing and a temporary suspension while the administration investigated the "intent."

This situation escalated because digital text lacks non-verbal cues like facial expressions and tone of voice. To avoid such fallout, the student should have followed the "Seconds Rule": waiting ten seconds before hitting send to evaluate if the message could be misconstrued. Additionally, sensitive or critical feedback should always be handled via voice call or in-person meetings. Clear communication is not just about the words used, but ensuring the recipient’s environment and state of mind are considered before broadcasting an opinion.
