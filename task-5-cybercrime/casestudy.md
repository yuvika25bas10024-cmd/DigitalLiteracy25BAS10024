**Case Study: UPI "Collect Request" & QR Code Fraud**

What the Crime Is

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a form of social engineering where scammers exploit the speed and convenience of digital payments to trick users into authorizing outgoing transactions. Unlike technical "hacking," this crime "hacks" the human user by manipulating their trust or lack of technical knowledge.

How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1.	Lure: A scammer contacts a student posing as a buyer on a platform like OLX or as a "scholarship officer." They agree to send money immediately.
2.	The Bait: The fraudster sends a small "test" amount (e.g., ₹5) to gain the victim's trust.
3.	The Trap: The fraudster sends a "Collect Request" or a QR Code via WhatsApp. They tell the student, "Scan this and enter your PIN to receive the remaining ₹10,000".
4.	The Execution: Once the student enters their UPI PIN, they have unknowingly authorized a payment to the scammer rather than receiving one. Money is instantly deducted from their bank account.

Who Is Usually Targeted

While anyone using digital payments is at risk, college students and young freelancers are frequent targets because they often engage in peer-to-peer selling, gig work, or are looking for quick financial aid/scholarships.

The Consequences

Victims face immediate financial loss, ranging from a few thousand to several lakhs of rupees. In a real 2025 incident, an IISc professor lost ₹63,000 while trying to sell an appliance online. Beyond the money, victims often experience significant psychological stress and a loss of trust in digital systems.
